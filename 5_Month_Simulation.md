# Month 1-5 Simulation: Firsthold (Planet Faldoran)

## Overview
It has been five months since the devastating goblin ambush that claimed the lives of ten settlers. In the wake of this tragedy, **Kyrill Stormbrow**, the strongest of the dwarven fighters, stepped up and assumed the role of **Mayor**. Under his stern, practical, and heavily fortified approach to leadership, mortality has been kept to an absolute minimum. The camp has evolved from a frantic group of survivors into a functional, militarized frontier village now officially named **Firsthold**.

---

## 🏗️ Building and Fortifications
Under Mayor Stormbrow’s command, survival and defense became the absolute priorities. 

*   **The Palisade (Months 1-2):** Led by builders like Benn Holt and the dwarven stoneworkers, a sturdy wooden palisade and earthen berm were erected around the camp's perimeter. The gate is now heavily guarded.
*   **The Crafter's Block (Months 2-3):** With Bram Corley (Weaponsmith) and Frida Anvilborn working the forge, and Petra Sull (Leatherworker) handling hides, a centralized crafting district was established to ensure the camp's defenders are well-equipped.
*   **Farming and Foraging (Months 3-5):** Recognizing the threat of starvation (a Cause of Death for some, ensuring they don't repeat the mistake), the farmers (Tomas Bell, Gregor Shaw, Mira Tealeaf) secured a section of fertile land just outside the gates, protected by continuous guard rotations. Small-scale crops and domesticated local wildlife now provide a stable food source.
*   **The Healing Pavilion:** Mara Thorne, Father Helmon, and the other clerics and adepts have established a triage and recovery area. Ambient magic has accelerated natural healing, and their magical abilities are heavily rationed.

---

## ⚔️ Activities and Advancements
Because of the Divine Preserve's highly concentrated ambient magic and constant exposure to deadly threats, five months of labor and fighting have hardened the populace. The **Lie of Power** is real—the accelerated growth means survivors are leveling much faster than they ever could on their homeworlds.

*   **Militia Training:** Kyrill mandated that everyone, regardless of their prior profession, learn basic self-defense. Fighters like Aldric Vane and Eryndor Lightblade run daily drills. 
*   **Scout Patrols:** Rangers and Rogues like Dain Mercer, Edrin Shaw, and Arannis Shade operate as the camp's eyes and ears. They carefully map the shifting, chaotic biomes. Here, the **Artificial Ecology** creates impossible borders—like a blazing desert abruptly giving way to a frozen tundra. They mark "death zones" where displaced apex predators roam, strictly avoiding large encounters to focus on gathering intel.
*   **Rapid Advancement & Limits:** Due to the extraordinary XP yield of surviving in the preserve, characters absorb ambient magic. However, mortal forms can only sustain a maximum gain of **100 XP per month**. Thus, surviving combatants have banked around 500 XP over these 5 months. While not yet enough to level up, they are close to reaching level 2 and have noticeably sharpened their respective skills.
*   **Echoes of Death:** Since everyone arrived here by dying, their former **Causes of Death** have manifested as ingrained psychological flaws and unexpected physical boons. For instance, villagers who originally drowned now possess a crippling phobia of the unnatural lakes but can hold their breath twice as long. Those who starved to death meticulously guard the camp's rationing lines and rarely succumb to fatigue. These traumatic quirks heavily influence daily assignments and guard duties.

## 🤝 Relationships and Factions
The goblin attack forged a strong bond through shared trauma, but as survival becomes more stable, distinct social dynamics have begun to emerge:

**1. The Shield-Brethren (The Core Military)**
*   **Key Figures:** Mayor Kyrill Stormbrow, Garin Stoutshield, shale Marr, Aldric Vane.
*   **Dynamic:** Practical, tough, and fiercely loyal to Kyrill. They believe security trumps all other concerns and favor heavily armed patrols and zero-tolerance policies on insubordination or wandering off.

**2. The Seekers (Scouts & Casters)**
*   **Key Figures:** Sylrieth Moonfall, Maris Venn, Dain Mercer, Jessa Clay.
*   **Dynamic:** The arcane casters and rangers are fascinated and terrified by the artificial ecology. They often clash with Kyrill over wanting to explore further and study the anomalous magic and biome borders. They act as the progressive wing of the camp, urging proactive scouting over defensive turtling.

**3. The Hearth-Keepers (Crafters & Providers)**
*   **Key Figures:** Bram Corley, Lysa Moor, Naivara Softstep, Hal Brennor.
*   **Dynamic:** They represent the heart of the camp. Their influence has grown because they control the food supply and equipment. They are generally supportive of Kyrill but are exhausted by the constant rationing and high production demands. 

**4. Interpersonal Developments & The First Families:**
*   **Dwarven Respect:** The dwarves have gained immense respect from all races for orchestrating the camp's physical defenses. Kyrill's leadership is universally acknowledged, though some find him draconian.
*   **Elven Adaptation:** The elves, initially horrified by the corruption of nature in this unnatural realm, have begun adapting their druidic and ranger talents to bend the hostile environment to their advantage. Thalion Greenbough has successfully cultivated several strange, fast-growing alien plants for food.
*   **Gnome/Halfling Ingenuity:** The smaller races, utilizing their natural stealth and tinkering abilities, have created exceptional traps surrounding the perimeter, keeping many predators at bay without needing direct confrontation.
*   **New Bonds:** Recognizing that their old lives are truly gone, people have begun forming romantic relationships to seek comfort and build a future. Several couples have emerged:
    *   **Tomas Bell (Farmer) & Marda Kell (Baker):** Bonded over securing the town's food supply.
    *   **Bram Corley (Weaponsmith) & Petra Sull (Leatherworker):** Fell in love working side-by-side in the Crafter's Block.
    *   **Dain Mercer (Ranger) & Sylrieth Moonfall (Wizard):** Brought together during perilous scout patrols.
    *   **Kyrill Stormbrow (Mayor) & Helja Coppervein (Cleric):** A practical, strong union at the head of the town's leadership.

## Summary 
The settlement is surviving, having carved out a fragile stability. They are better equipped, more experienced, and unified under Mayor Kyrill. With relationships blooming, they are laying the foundation for a true town. However, they are now painfully aware of the artificial nature of the world. They know something terrifying could crash through the treeline, or a temporal anomaly could warp their reality at any moment.
